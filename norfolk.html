A Navy town deserves Navy-aware care

Care that understands the uniform

Norfolk is home to Naval Station Norfolk, the largest naval base in the world, with the fleet, the piers, and the shipyard work that keep it running. That means a lot of people here carry the weight of service and the pressure of an unpredictable schedule. At Anchor Neuro, military life is not an abstraction. We get the deployments, the duty rotations, and the very real fear that asking for help could follow you.

Who you would be working with

Led by a military spouse who gets it

Courtney Davis is a board-certified psychiatric nurse practitioner and is board-certified in neurofeedback, with her doctorate from Old Dominion University, right here in Norfolk. She is also the spouse of an Army veteran, so the rhythm and cost of military life are familiar ground, not a talking point.

How care actually reaches Norfolk

Two honest ways to be seen

To be clear, our office is in Virginia Beach, not Norfolk. We reach the Norfolk area two ways, and both are real. First, secure telehealth anywhere in Virginia, which is a good fit for tight sailor and shift schedules, for sea duty turnarounds, and for the privacy of meeting from home instead of a waiting room. Second, in-person visits at the Virginia Beach office, a short drive from Norfolk.

Most of the care can happen either way. Medication management and most trauma-focused therapy work well by telehealth or in person. Neurofeedback, the complementary option, is the exception. It is provided in the office only, so if neurofeedback is part of your plan, you would come to Virginia Beach for those sessions.

If you are in crisis right now, you do not have to wait. Call the Veterans Crisis Line at 988 and press 1, or text 838255. Anchor Neuro is not a crisis or emergency service.

The thing most people ask first

Discretion, answered straight

For many service members, the biggest barrier to care is not the symptoms. It is the fear that getting help will follow you, into your career, your clearance, or your standing with your unit. That fear is real, and it deserves a straight answer, not a sales pitch.

Here is the part that applies to any provider: your health information is confidential and protected by law through secure, HIPAA-compliant systems. There are narrow legal and safety exceptions that exist for everyone, and we are glad to walk you through exactly what is and is not shared before you begin. The details around active-duty care and command notification can depend on your status and setting, so we would rather have that conversation honestly with you than make a blanket promise.

What we offer

Services, woven into one plan

There is no single fix. Medication management and trauma care lead the way, combined into one plan that fits you, and we are honest about what each part can and cannot do.

  1. Psychiatric medication management. A thorough evaluation, thoughtful prescribing when it fits, and ongoing follow-up and adjustment, collaborative and measured. Start low, go slow, and you have a say. More on medication management.
  2. Trauma & PTSD care. Paced, safety-first care for combat and service-related trauma and beyond, through trauma-focused therapy, built around trust before we go deep. See our trauma & PTSD treatment.
  3. Brainspotting. One of the trauma-focused therapy methods Courtney is trained in, a brain-body approach with growing clinical use including with veterans, though its research base is still developing. More on Brainspotting.
  4. Neurofeedback. A complementary, medication-free option offered alongside medication and therapy. Sensors only read your activity; nothing is sent into your brain. Provided in the Virginia Beach office only.

These work best as part of an integrated plan, and medication works best alongside therapy, which Courtney can provide or help coordinate. Prescribing decisions, including for controlled medications, are made individually after a clinical evaluation and follow Virginia and federal rules; some may require an in-person visit or added monitoring. We will always be honest about what to expect, and we will not promise to fix or cure trauma.

Honest answers

Questions from the Norfolk area

Do you have an office in Norfolk?

No. Anchor Neuro has one office, in the Hilltop area of Virginia Beach, and we are honest about that. We reach the Norfolk area two ways: secure telehealth anywhere in Virginia, and in-person visits at our Virginia Beach office, which is a short drive from Norfolk. If you want EEG neurofeedback, that is provided in the office only, so you would come to Virginia Beach for those sessions.

Will my command or chain of command find out I am getting care?

Your care here is private and protected by law through secure, HIPAA-compliant systems. We are not part of your chain of command, and our role is your care, not reporting to your unit. As with any provider, there are narrow legal and safety exceptions that apply to everyone, and rules around active-duty care and command notification can depend on your status and setting. We will walk you through exactly what is and is not shared before you begin, so you can decide with a clear picture.

To confirm before launch (legal review): exact "will my command find out" wording and how command-notification rules differ by duty status and care setting.

I work shifts and sea duty. Can telehealth fit a Navy schedule?

That is much of why telehealth exists here. Secure video visits across Virginia let you meet from home or somewhere private without a drive across the tunnel, which helps when your hours are unpredictable or you value privacy. Medication management and most trauma-focused therapy can be done by telehealth or in person. Neurofeedback, the complementary option, is in-office only, so those sessions happen at the Virginia Beach office.

Do you work with veterans and active-duty service members?

Yes. Anchor Neuro provides discreet, trauma-informed care for service members, veterans, and their families, and is a member of The Headstrong Project. Courtney is the spouse of an Army veteran and understands military life and the discretion that trust requires.

Do you take TRICARE?

Please ask us first. We are in-network with Aetna, Anthem / HealthKeepers, BlueCross BlueShield, Carelon, Cigna / Evernorth, and Sentara, and we offer self-pay. If you carry TRICARE or military coverage, reach out and we will tell you where things stand and walk through your options before you start, never as a surprise afterward.

To confirm before launch: TRICARE and Humana Military network / credentialing status before any in-network claim is made.

Can neurofeedback or Brainspotting cure PTSD?

No, and we will not promise a cure. Research supports neurofeedback for attention and for stress and anxiety regulation; for PTSD and trauma specifically, the evidence is still emerging and promising rather than definitive. Brainspotting is a newer brain-body trauma modality with growing clinical use, including with veterans, but a still-developing research base. We use them as part of an integrated, paced plan, not as a guarantee.
