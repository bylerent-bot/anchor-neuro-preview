PTSD & Trauma Treatment in Virginia Beach

Discreet, trauma-informed care for veterans, service members, and their families, and for anyone carrying the weight of what they have been through. We move at a pace that feels safe, and we are honest about what helps.

Safety first, always

A paced, trauma-informed approach

Trauma does not heal on a deadline, and pushing too fast can do more harm than good. At Anchor Neuro, we build trust before we go deep. We start by helping your nervous system feel steady, then move into the harder work only when you are ready. You set the pace. We meet you where you are, in a calm space where you can finally exhale.

Care that understands service

The military community, done right

Hampton Roads carries one of the largest active-duty and veteran populations in the country, with Naval Station Norfolk, NAS Oceana, and Joint Expeditionary Base Little Creek close by. Many of the people who serve here put off care, not because they do not need it, but because they worry about who might find out.

Courtney is the spouse of an Army veteran, so military life is not an abstraction here. She knows the rhythm of deployments, the culture, and the cost of carrying it all. Anchor Neuro is a member of The Headstrong Project, a veterans' mental-health program, and provides discreet care for service members, veterans, and their families.

You do not have to be combat-deployed, diagnosed, or at a breaking point to reach out. If something you carry is getting in the way of your life, that is reason enough.

The thing most people ask first

Discretion you can count on

For many service members, the number-one barrier to care is not the symptoms. It is the fear that getting help will follow you, into your career, your clearance, or your standing with your unit. That fear is real, and it deserves a straight answer, not a sales pitch.

Here is the universal truth that applies to any provider: your health information is confidential and protected by law through secure, HIPAA-compliant systems. There are narrow legal and safety exceptions that exist for everyone, and we are glad to walk you through exactly what is and is not shared before you begin. The details around active-duty care and command notification can depend on your status and setting, so we would rather have that conversation honestly with you than make a blanket promise.

More than one tool

Treatment options, woven together

There is no single fix for trauma. We combine approaches into one plan that fits you, and we are honest about what each one can and cannot do.

  1. Trauma-informed psychiatric care. A careful evaluation and a relationship that feels safe, with care paced to your readiness. This is the foundation everything else builds on. See our integrative psychiatric care.
  2. EEG neurofeedback. A non-invasive, medication-free way to help your brain settle into calmer patterns. Research supports it for attention and for stress and anxiety regulation; for PTSD and trauma the evidence is still emerging and promising. Sensors only read your activity, nothing is sent into your brain. Learn about neurofeedback in Virginia Beach (in-office only).
  3. Brainspotting. A newer brain-body trauma modality that Courtney is trained in and uses, with growing clinical use including with veterans, though its research base is still developing. More on Brainspotting.
  4. Medication, when it helps. Used thoughtfully and only when clinically appropriate, never as a one-size-fits-all default and never a guarantee.

These work best as part of an integrated plan. We will always be honest with you about what to expect, and we will not promise to fix or cure trauma.

Honest answers

Questions about trauma & PTSD care

Will my command find out I am getting care?

Your care here is private and protected by law. We are not part of your chain of command, and our role is your care, not reporting to your unit. Like any health provider, there are narrow legal and safety exceptions that apply to everyone, and rules around active-duty care and command notification can depend on your status and setting. We will walk you through exactly what is and is not shared before you begin, so you can decide with a clear picture.

To confirm before launch (legal review): exact "will my command find out" wording and how command-notification rules differ by duty status and care setting.

Do you work with veterans and active-duty service members?

Yes. Anchor Neuro provides discreet, trauma-informed care for service members, veterans, and their families, and is a member of The Headstrong Project. Courtney is the spouse of an Army veteran and understands military life and the discretion that trust requires.

Can neurofeedback and Brainspotting really help with trauma?

They may help, and we are honest about what each can and cannot do. Research supports neurofeedback for attention and for stress and anxiety regulation; for PTSD and trauma specifically, the evidence is still emerging and promising rather than definitive. Brainspotting is a newer brain-body trauma modality with growing clinical use, including with veterans, but a still-developing research base. We use them as part of an integrated plan, not as a cure or a guarantee.

Do you take TRICARE?

Please ask us. We are in-network with Aetna, Anthem / HealthKeepers, BlueCross BlueShield, Carelon, Cigna / Evernorth, and Sentara, and we offer self-pay. If you carry TRICARE or military coverage, reach out and we will tell you where things stand and walk through your options before you start, never as a surprise afterward.

To confirm before launch: TRICARE and Humana Military network / credentialing status before any in-network claim is made.

How fast does trauma treatment work?

There is no fixed timeline, and we will not promise one. Trauma care works best when it is paced and safety-first, and we move at a speed that feels right for you. Some people feel steadier early on; deeper work takes time. We build the plan with you and adjust as you go.
