A brain-body approach to trauma

Brainspotting in Virginia Beach

Some things don't move with talk alone. Brainspotting is a gentle, brain-body way of processing trauma and distress, used at Anchor Neuro alongside neurofeedback and psychiatric care. We will be honest with you about what it can and cannot do.

What it is

Where you look can change how you feel

Brainspotting is built on a simple observation: the direction your eyes rest in can connect to where a feeling or memory is held in your brain and body. Developed by Dr. David Grand, it helps you find and gently process the places talk therapy can struggle to reach, working with deeper, body-based parts of the brain rather than just the thinking, language part.

It is honest work, not magic. Brainspotting is a newer modality with a growing but still-developing research base. Courtney is trained in it and uses it because many people, including veterans, find it helpful, and because it fits how she practices: paced, individualized, and led by you. It is not a guaranteed cure, and it works best as part of an integrated plan rather than on its own.

An honest note on the evidence. Brainspotting is an emerging brain-body therapy with promising clinical use and a research base that is still developing. We use it as a tool, not a promise, and we will always be straight with you about what to expect.

What a session is like

Calm, paced, and led by you

A Brainspotting session is quiet and unhurried. You stay in control the whole time, and nothing is forced. We move only as fast as feels safe.

  1. We get grounded. You settle in, and we connect to what you want to work on, at a pace that feels right.
  2. We find a spot. With gentle guidance, you notice where your eyes naturally rest while you stay with a feeling or memory.
  3. Your brain does the work. You let your brain and body process from that spot, while your provider stays with you and tracks how you are doing.
  4. We close gently. We bring the session to a calm, steady finish and talk through what came up and what comes next.

Every session is trauma-informed and client-led. You decide what to share and how far to go.

How it fits together

One plan, built around you

Common questions

Brainspotting, answered honestly

Is Brainspotting a proven cure for trauma or PTSD?

No, and we will not tell you otherwise. Brainspotting is a newer brain-body modality with a growing but still-developing research base. It is used clinically with many people, including veterans, and many find it helpful. It is not a guaranteed cure, and we are honest about what it can and cannot do. We use it as one part of an integrated, individualized plan.

What is a Brainspotting session like?

Sessions are calm, paced, and client-led. With your provider's guidance, you notice where your eyes naturally rest while you stay connected to a feeling or memory, and you let your brain and body process at their own speed. You stay in control the whole time. Nothing is forced, and we move only as fast as feels safe for you.

Who can Brainspotting help?

People come to Brainspotting for trauma and PTSD, anxiety, grief and loss, and performance blocks that talk alone has not shifted. It is used in trauma work with veterans and service members, which is part of why it fits Anchor Neuro's mission. Whether it is right for you is something we decide together after we understand your history and goals.

How does Brainspotting fit with neurofeedback and medication?

It works best as part of an integrated plan. Brainspotting can pair with EEG neurofeedback, which helps your brain practice steadier patterns, and with psychiatric care and medication management when those help. Courtney builds one plan around you rather than treating each piece in isolation.

Is Brainspotting available by telehealth?

We offer Brainspotting in person in Virginia Beach and, where clinically appropriate, by secure telehealth across Virginia. The right setting depends on what you are working on and what feels safe for you. We will talk it through during your free consult.
