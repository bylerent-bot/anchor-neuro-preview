A trauma-focused therapy method

Brainspotting in Virginia Beach

Some things don't move with talk alone. Brainspotting is a gentle, brain-body way of processing trauma, and it is one of the trauma-focused therapy methods we coordinate and connect you with as part of your trauma and PTSD care, alongside Courtney's medication management. We refer you to a trusted, experienced therapist who provides it, and we will be honest with you about what it can and cannot do.

What it is

Where you look can change how you feel

Brainspotting is built on a simple observation: the direction your eyes rest in can connect to where a feeling or memory is held in your brain and body. Developed by Dr. David Grand, it helps you find and gently process the places talk therapy can struggle to reach, working with deeper, body-based parts of the brain rather than just the thinking, language part.

It is honest work, not magic. Brainspotting is a newer modality with a growing but still-developing research base. Anchor Neuro coordinates it and refers you to a trusted, experienced therapist who provides it, because many people, including veterans, find it helpful, and because it fits how we practice: paced, individualized, and led by you. Courtney manages the medication side herself and stays your consistent point of contact through it all. It is not a guaranteed cure, and it works best as part of an integrated plan, used within trauma care and alongside medication management, rather than on its own.

An honest note on the evidence. Brainspotting is an emerging brain-body therapy with promising clinical use and a research base that is still developing. We treat it as a tool, not a promise, coordinating it and referring you to a trusted therapist who provides it, and we will always be straight with you about what to expect.

What a session is like

Calm, paced, and led by you

A Brainspotting session is quiet and unhurried. You stay in control the whole time, and nothing is forced. Your therapist moves only as fast as feels safe. Anchor Neuro coordinates the referral so the right trained therapist is matched to you.

  1. You get grounded. You settle in, and you and your therapist connect to what you want to work on, at a pace that feels right.
  2. You find a spot. With your therapist's gentle guidance, you notice where your eyes naturally rest while you stay with a feeling or memory.
  3. Your brain does the work. You let your brain and body process from that spot, while your therapist stays with you and tracks how you are doing.
  4. You close gently. Your therapist brings the session to a calm, steady finish and talks through what came up and what comes next.

Every session is trauma-informed and client-led. You decide what to share and how far to go.

Common questions

Brainspotting, answered honestly

Is Brainspotting a proven cure for trauma or PTSD?

No, and we will not tell you otherwise. Brainspotting is a newer brain-body modality with a growing but still-developing research base. It is used clinically with many people, including veterans, and many find it helpful. It is not a guaranteed cure, and we are honest about what it can and cannot do. We coordinate it as one part of an integrated, individualized plan and refer you to a trusted therapist who provides it.

What is a Brainspotting session like?

Sessions are calm, paced, and client-led. With your therapist's guidance, you notice where your eyes naturally rest while you stay connected to a feeling or memory, and you let your brain and body process at their own speed. You stay in control the whole time. Nothing is forced, and your therapist moves only as fast as feels safe for you. Anchor Neuro coordinates Brainspotting and refers you to a trained, trusted therapist who provides it, while Courtney manages your medication side and stays your consistent point of contact.

Who can Brainspotting help?

People come to Brainspotting for trauma and PTSD, anxiety, grief and loss, and performance blocks that talk alone has not shifted. It is used in trauma work with veterans and service members, which is part of why it fits Anchor Neuro's mission. Whether it is right for you is something we decide together after we understand your history and goals, and we connect you with a trusted therapist who provides it.

How does Brainspotting fit with medication and trauma care?

Brainspotting is a trauma-focused therapy that works best as part of an integrated plan. Anchor Neuro coordinates it and refers you to a trusted, experienced therapist who provides it, while Courtney manages the psychiatric medication side herself and stays your consistent point of contact within your trauma and PTSD care. Neurofeedback is available in office as a complementary option for those who want it. Courtney builds one plan around you rather than leaving you to piece care together.

Is Brainspotting available by telehealth?

Brainspotting is a trauma therapy we can connect you with through trusted referral partners and coordinate as part of your plan. The referred therapist may offer it in person in Virginia Beach and, where clinically appropriate, by secure telehealth across Virginia. The right setting depends on what you are working on and what feels safe for you. We will talk it through during your free consult.
