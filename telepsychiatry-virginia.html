Care that comes to you

Good mental health care, no matter where you are in Virginia

Getting to an appointment is its own hurdle. A long drive, a day off work, finding someone to watch the kids, or a base schedule that never holds still. Telehealth removes a lot of that. If you are physically located anywhere in Virginia, you can meet with us by secure video from home, from your office, or from somewhere private when you are on the road.

This is the same whole-person care we offer in Virginia Beach, just over a screen. We start by listening, take time to understand your history and your goals, and build a plan around you rather than reaching for a quick label. Telehealth is not a watered-down version of care. For many people it is simply the version that finally fits their life.

What works by video, and what doesn't

What is available by telehealth

Most of what we do works well over a secure video visit. Some things still need the office. Here is the honest breakdown so you know what to expect.

  • Medication management. A thorough psychiatric evaluation, thoughtful prescribing when it fits, and ongoing follow-up and adjustment, all by secure video when that suits you. We work collaboratively and measured, often "start low, go slow," and you have a say. Medication is one option within a plan, and it works best alongside therapy. Learn more about medication management.
  • Follow-up psychiatric care. Ongoing care that looks at the whole picture of what is going on, with regular check-ins to see how you are responding, available anywhere in Virginia.
  • Trauma-focused therapy. Trauma care, including Brainspotting, a brain-body approach to processing difficult experiences, which can be done by secure video or in person. Learn more about trauma care.

Prescribing decisions, including for controlled medications, are made individually after a clinical evaluation and follow Virginia and federal rules; some may require an in-person visit or added monitoring. We do not promise any specific medication in advance, and there are no guarantees, only a careful look at what fits you.

To confirm before launch: your exact controlled-substance policy by telehealth (which categories you will and will not prescribe online, and when an in-person visit is required).

Neurofeedback is in-office only. It uses sensors and equipment that have to be set up in person at our Virginia Beach office, so it cannot be done online. The sensors only read your brain activity; nothing is sent into your brain. Many people combine video visits with occasional in-person neurofeedback sessions.

Private by design

Your visit stays private

Telehealth visits happen on a HIPAA-compliant, secure video platform, not a casual app or a personal video call. To keep things private on your end, find a quiet space where you will not be overheard, use headphones if you can, and make sure you have a stable internet connection. A phone, tablet, or computer all work.

For service members and military families especially, telehealth offers a level of discretion that an in-person visit sometimes cannot: care from your own home, scheduled around duty and deployments, without a waiting room. If you are weighing how this fits with active-duty life, we are glad to talk it through.

Questions, answered honestly

Telehealth FAQ

Where do I have to be located for a telehealth visit?

You need to be physically located in Virginia at the time of your visit. Telehealth licensing rules are based on where you are sitting during the appointment, not where you live or where we are. So you can be at home in Roanoke, in an office in Richmond, or visiting family in Northern Virginia, as long as you are in the Commonwealth. If you travel or move out of state, let us know so we can talk through your options.

Is neurofeedback available online?

No. Neurofeedback is in-office only, at our Virginia Beach office on Laskin Road, because it uses sensors and equipment that have to be set up in person. The sensors only read your brain activity, nothing is sent into your brain. Psychiatric care, medication management, and Brainspotting can all be done by secure video, so many people combine online visits with occasional in-person neurofeedback sessions.

What can I actually do by telehealth versus in person?

By secure video anywhere in Virginia, you can do integrative psychiatric evaluations, ongoing medication management, and Brainspotting sessions. In person at our Virginia Beach office you can do those same services, plus neurofeedback, which is in-office only. Many people mix the two: video for routine follow-ups and in-person visits when it helps.

Is telehealth private and secure?

Yes. Visits happen on a HIPAA-compliant, secure video platform, not a casual app like a personal video call. For privacy, find a quiet space where you will not be overheard, use headphones if you can, and have a stable internet connection. For service members and military families, telehealth can offer added discretion and scheduling flexibility around a demanding routine.

Is telehealth right for everyone?

Telehealth fits a lot of situations, but not all of them. Some needs are better served in person, and some are outside what an outpatient practice can safely handle by video. We will be honest with you about that. Anchor Neuro is not a crisis or emergency service. If you are in crisis or thinking about harming yourself, call or text 988 (Suicide and Crisis Lifeline; veterans press 1) or go to your nearest emergency room.
