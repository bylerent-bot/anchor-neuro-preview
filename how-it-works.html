How the free consult works

Your first step is a short, no-pressure consult, free of charge. It is a brief conversation to see whether we are the right fit for each other. It is not a clinical appointment, diagnosis, or medication visit.

To make the most of it, we ask you to complete a short, secure request form first. We review every request personally and reach out to schedule your consult if we look like a good match, or help point you elsewhere if we are not. There is no instant booking for new clients, because we want every consult to count.

The path of care

Most people come to Anchor Neuro for psychiatric medication management, trauma care, or both. Care follows a simple, steady arc:

1. A thorough evaluation. We take the time to understand your history, your symptoms, your goals, and what you have already tried, so any plan is built on a real picture of you rather than a quick label.

2. A plan that fits. Together we decide what makes sense: medication when it fits, trauma-focused therapy such as Brainspotting, or a combination. Medication tends to work best alongside therapy. Courtney manages the medication side herself and coordinates your therapy, connecting you with a trusted, experienced therapist, so you are not piecing care together alone.

3. Medication and/or therapy. If medication is part of the plan, we prescribe thoughtfully and start conservatively. If trauma therapy is part of the plan, we coordinate it and connect you with a trusted therapist who provides it, at a pace that's right for you. Neurofeedback is available in-office as a complementary, medication-free option some people choose to add.

4. Follow-up and adjustment. We check in over time and adjust based on how you are actually doing. Care is collaborative, and you always have a say in the next step.

How medication management works

Medication management here means a thorough psychiatric evaluation, thoughtful prescribing when it fits, and ongoing follow-up to see how you are responding. We work in a measured way, start low and go slow, and you stay part of every decision. We do not promise that the right medication will fix everything; medication is one option within a plan, and therapy and neurofeedback are others. It often works best alongside therapy.

We do not guarantee any specific medication or outcome, do not provide early refills, and may request prior records as part of safe, responsible care.

Prescribing decisions, including for controlled medications, are made individually after a clinical evaluation and follow Virginia and federal rules. Some may require an in-person visit or added monitoring. We do not advertise or promise stimulants, benzodiazepines, or any specific controlled medication.

Your privacy

Your health information is kept confidential and handled through secure, HIPAA-compliant systems. Discretion matters to us, especially for those who carry the weight of service. Anything you share in your request form stays protected.

Anchor Neuro is not a crisis or emergency service. If you are in crisis or thinking about harming yourself, call or text 988 (Suicide and Crisis Lifeline; veterans press 1) or go to your nearest emergency room.
