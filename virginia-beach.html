Where to find us

Anchor Neuro has one office, and it is here in Virginia Beach: 1206 Laskin Rd, Suite 201-G, Virginia Beach, VA 23451. We are in the Hilltop area near First Colonial Rd, an easy reach from the Oceanfront and the rest of the city.

In person or by telehealth

Two ways to be seen

You can come into the Virginia Beach office, or you can meet by secure telehealth from anywhere in Virginia. Courtney's psychiatric medication management works well either way, and the trauma therapy she coordinates and refers you to can usually be arranged in person or by telehealth through a trusted therapist, so you can choose what fits your week.

Neurofeedback, offered as a complementary option, is the one service that is in-office only. The sensors and equipment have to be with you in the room, so telehealth clients who add neurofeedback come to Virginia Beach for those sessions. We will always tell you up front when an in-person visit is needed, so nothing is a surprise.

Who we serve from here

Virginia Beach, Hampton Roads, and the military community

From this one office and through telehealth, we care for people across the region, with particular care for those who serve.

This office serves Virginia Beach and the greater Hampton Roads area. Hampton Roads carries one of the largest military footprints in the country, with Naval Station Norfolk, NAS Oceana right here in Virginia Beach, and JEB Little Creek nearby. As the spouse of an Army veteran and a member of The Headstrong Project, Courtney offers discreet, trauma-informed care for service members, veterans, and their families.

A note on coverage: we are in-network with several major plans, but we are not credentialed with TRICARE. We would confirm your coverage or a clear self-pay rate before your first visit.

What we offer here

Care at the Virginia Beach office

Courtney's medication management leads the way, with trauma therapy coordinated and referred to a trusted therapist, and telehealth available for all of it except in-office neurofeedback.

Psychiatric medication management

A thorough evaluation, thoughtful prescribing when it fits, and ongoing follow-up and adjustment, in person or by telehealth. Start low, go slow, and you have a say.

Trauma & PTSD care

Paced, trauma-informed psychiatric medication management for service members, veterans, families, and anyone carrying the past, with trauma therapy coordinated and referred to a trusted therapist.

Brainspotting

One of the trauma-focused therapy methods we coordinate and connect you with through trusted referral partners, a brain-body approach to processing trauma, offered as part of your coordinated trauma care.

Neurofeedback

A complementary, medication-free option offered alongside medication and therapy. In-office only, here in Virginia Beach.

Cost & coverage

Insurance and self-pay, no surprises

We accept insurance and self-pay. We are in-network for psychiatric care with Aetna, Anthem / HealthKeepers, BlueCross BlueShield, Carelon, Cigna / Evernorth, and Sentara. We confirm your coverage or a clear self-pay rate before your first visit, never as a surprise afterward.

  • Psychiatric medication management, in person or by telehealth across Virginia
  • Trauma and PTSD care, with neurofeedback offered as a complementary option
  • New clients welcome
  • Discreet care for military families

Prescribing decisions, including for controlled medications, are made individually after a clinical evaluation and follow Virginia and federal rules. Some may require an in-person visit or added monitoring. Medication works best alongside therapy; Courtney manages the medication side herself, coordinates your therapy, and refers you to a trusted, experienced therapist, so you are not piecing care together alone, and we never promise that the right medication will fix everything.

Questions, answered

Visiting Anchor Neuro

Where exactly is your Virginia Beach office?

We are at 1206 Laskin Rd, Suite 201-G, Virginia Beach, VA 23451, in the Hilltop area near First Colonial Rd, a few minutes from the Oceanfront. When you arrive you will find a quiet, calm space. There is a map on this page to help you find us.

Do I have to come in person?

For most of what we do, no. Courtney's psychiatric medication management can be done by secure telehealth anywhere in Virginia, or in person at our Virginia Beach office, whichever you prefer, and the trauma therapy she coordinates and refers you to can usually be arranged by telehealth or in person through a trusted therapist. The one exception is neurofeedback, our complementary in-office option, because the sensors and equipment have to be with you in the room. Clients who add neurofeedback come to Virginia Beach for those sessions.

Do you serve areas outside Virginia Beach?

Yes. Our only office is in Virginia Beach, and we serve the rest of Hampton Roads two ways. Secure telehealth reaches anyone located in Virginia, so people in Norfolk, Chesapeake, and beyond can be seen without driving. And the office is a short drive for anyone in the region who prefers to come in, or who is adding in-office neurofeedback. We do not have a second location anywhere else.

What insurance do you accept?

We are in-network for psychiatric care with Aetna, Anthem / HealthKeepers, BlueCross BlueShield, Carelon, Cigna / Evernorth, and Sentara, and we also see self-pay clients. We confirm your coverage or a clear self-pay rate before your first visit, never as a surprise afterward.

Do you work with the military community?

Yes. As the spouse of an Army veteran and a member of The Headstrong Project, Courtney provides discreet care for service members, veterans, and their families across Hampton Roads, from Naval Station Norfolk to NAS Oceana to JEB Little Creek. Please note we are not credentialed with TRICARE, so we would confirm your coverage or self-pay rate up front.

Anchor Neuro is not a crisis or emergency service. If you are in crisis or thinking about harming yourself, call or text 988 (Suicide and Crisis Lifeline; veterans press 1) or go to your nearest emergency room.
