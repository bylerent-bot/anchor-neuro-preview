The Anchor difference

What is neurofeedback?

Neurofeedback is a non-invasive, medication-free approach that helps your brain learn to regulate itself. Using sensors that read your brain's electrical activity, it gives your brain gentle, real-time feedback, so over time it can settle into calmer, more focused patterns on its own.

  1. We listen first. A thorough assessment of your history, symptoms, and goals.
  2. We map your brain's patterns. Sensors read your activity — completely painless, nothing is sent into you.
  3. Your brain practices. Through relaxing sessions, it's gently guided toward healthier rhythms.
  4. Change that lasts. With repetition, steadier patterns become your brain's new normal.

Research supports neurofeedback for attention, anxiety, and stress regulation. It works best as part of an integrated plan, and we'll always be honest with you about what it can and can't do.

Meet your provider

Courtney Davis, DNP, APRN, PMHNP-BC, BCN

Founder & Psychiatric Mental Health Nurse Practitioner

Dr. Courtney Davis is a board-certified Psychiatric Mental Health Nurse Practitioner and the founder of Anchor Neuro. She holds a Doctor of Nursing Practice (DNP) from Old Dominion University and is board-certified in neurofeedback through the Biofeedback Certification International Alliance.

She built Anchor Neuro on a simple belief: mental health care has to be individualized, because there is no one-size-fits-all path to healing. Courtney blends evidence-based psychiatric care and medication management with neuroscience-informed approaches like Brainspotting and EEG-guided neurofeedback, always in a safe, trauma-informed space where you feel heard.

As the spouse of an Army veteran, Courtney understands military life and the weight it can carry. She partners with The Headstrong Project, supporting service members, veterans, and their families with the discretion that trust requires.

As Anchor Neuro grows, Courtney is building a team of clinicians who share that philosophy, so more people across Hampton Roads can find steady, compassionate care.
