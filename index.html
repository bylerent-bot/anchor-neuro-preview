Our approach

Thoughtful medication management

Medication can be a powerful part of feeling better, but it's never a guess. Care starts with a thorough psychiatric evaluation, then careful prescribing only when it fits your goals, followed by regular follow-up to see how you're doing and adjust as needed.

  1. A real evaluation first. We take time to understand your history, symptoms, and what you want to feel different.
  2. Start low, go slow. When medication makes sense, we begin conservatively and change course based on how you actually respond.
  3. You have a say. Prescribing is a shared decision. We talk through options, benefits, and trade-offs together.
  4. Ongoing follow-up. We stay with you, fine-tuning the plan over time rather than setting it and walking away.

Medication works best alongside therapy, and it's one option within your plan, not the whole plan. Prescribing decisions, including for controlled medications, are made individually after a clinical evaluation and follow Virginia and federal rules; some may require an in-person visit or added monitoring. Meet your provider

Courtney Davis, DNP, APRN, PMHNP-BC, BCN

Founder & Psychiatric Mental Health Nurse Practitioner

Courtney Davis is a board-certified Psychiatric Mental Health Nurse Practitioner and the founder of Anchor Neuro. She holds a Doctor of Nursing Practice (DNP) from Old Dominion University and is board-certified in neurofeedback through the Biofeedback Certification International Alliance.

She built Anchor Neuro on a simple belief: mental health care has to be individualized, because there is no one-size-fits-all path to healing. Courtney's core work is thoughtful psychiatric medication management and trauma-informed care, with additional tools like Brainspotting and EEG-guided neurofeedback when they fit your goals, always in a safe space where you feel heard.

As the spouse of an Army veteran, Courtney understands military life and the weight it can carry. She partners with The Headstrong Project, supporting service members, veterans, and their families with the discretion that trust requires.

As Anchor Neuro grows, Courtney is building a team of clinicians who share that philosophy, so more people across Hampton Roads can find steady, compassionate care.
