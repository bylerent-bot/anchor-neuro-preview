What we treat

These are some of the most common reasons people reach out to Anchor Neuro. It is not a complete list. If what you are carrying isn't here, reach out anyway, and we'll help you find the right path or point you somewhere that fits.

Trauma & PTSD

Trauma can show up long after the event itself: flashbacks, a body that stays on high alert, sleep that won't come, numbness, or a sense that you're never quite safe. It can follow combat, an accident, loss, abuse, or a single hard moment that your nervous system never finished processing.

We offer trauma-informed care that moves at your pace and never pushes you to relive more than you're ready for. Depending on your goals, that may include Brainspotting, in-office EEG neurofeedback, medication when it's clinically appropriate, and steady psychiatric support. For trauma and PTSD specifically, the research on neurofeedback is still emerging and promising rather than settled, so we're honest about what it can and can't do and we use it as one part of a larger plan.

Anxiety

Anxiety can look like a mind that won't slow down, a chest that stays tight, worry that loops at 2 a.m., or avoiding the things you used to handle. For some people it's constant background static; for others it arrives in waves or full panic.

We start by understanding what's driving it, then build a plan around you. That might mean integrative psychiatric care and thoughtful medication management when it fits, in-office neurofeedback, which has reasonable research support for calming anxiety and stress, or a combination. The goal is steadier days, not a quick label.

Learn more about our psychiatric care →

Depression

Depression isn't only sadness. It can be a heaviness you can't shake, losing interest in things you used to love, trouble concentrating, changes in sleep or appetite, or just feeling flat and far away from your own life.

You deserve more than a rushed appointment and a prescription. Our psychiatric care looks at the whole picture, your history, your stress, your sleep, your relationships, and we build a plan together. Medication is one tool we use only when it's clinically appropriate, alongside other supports, and we check in over time to see whether things are genuinely moving in the right direction.

Learn more about our psychiatric care →

ADHD & focus

ADHD can mean a mind that jumps from thing to thing, trouble starting or finishing tasks, lost time, restlessness, or the exhausting work of holding it all together while it feels like everyone else just does it. It can affect work, focus, relationships, and everyday life.

We offer a careful evaluation and a plan that fits your life. In-office EEG neurofeedback has reasonable research support for attention and focus, and it's a medication-free option many people want to explore. When medication does make sense, we prescribe responsibly, only after a full evaluation, and we track how it's working.

Learn more about neurofeedback → or explore the broader option of psychiatric care.

Bipolar & mood disorders

Mood that swings between high-energy stretches and deep lows can be disorienting and hard to live with, for you and the people who love you. Bipolar and related mood disorders deserve careful, experienced attention rather than guesswork.

We take time to understand your patterns and history before we settle on anything. Our psychiatric care leans on responsible medication management when it's clinically appropriate, paired with steady support and honest check-ins so we can adjust based on how you're actually doing. We'll always be clear about what we can help with and when a higher level of care is the safer choice.

Learn more about our psychiatric care →

OCD

OCD can trap you in a loop: intrusive thoughts that won't leave, and rituals or checking that bring relief for a moment and then start over. It's tiring, it's often misunderstood, and it has nothing to do with simply being tidy or particular.

We offer compassionate psychiatric care, including thoughtful medication management when it's clinically appropriate, and we'll talk honestly about what fits your goals. When focused therapy approaches are the better path, we'll say so and help you find the right support rather than stretching ours past where it helps.

Learn more about our psychiatric care →

Grief & loss

Grief doesn't follow a schedule. It can come in waves, settle into a heaviness that lingers, or surface as anxiety, anger, or numbness long after a loss. Losing a person, a marriage, a career, or a future you'd counted on can all carry real weight.

We hold space for what you're going through and help you carry it, without rushing you to be okay. That can include supportive psychiatric care and, when it fits your goals, Brainspotting, a newer brain-body approach with a still-developing research base, used to work gently with grief and the way the body holds it.

Learn more about our psychiatric care → or explore Brainspotting.

Sleep & insomnia

When sleep falls apart, everything else gets harder: focus, mood, patience, your health. You might lie awake for hours, wake at 3 a.m. and can't get back, or sleep through the night and still feel wrecked in the morning.

We look at what's underneath the sleep problem, since anxiety, stress, mood, and trauma often sit at the root. In-office neurofeedback has reasonable research support for helping the nervous system settle and regulate, which can support better rest, and we pair it with whole-person care rather than reaching straight for a sleeping pill.

Learn more about neurofeedback → or explore the broader option of psychiatric care.

Stress & burnout

Burnout is what happens when you've run on empty for too long: exhausted but wired, short-fused, foggy, and detached from work and people you care about. It's common in Hampton Roads among military families, first responders, healthcare workers, and anyone carrying more than one full load.

We help you find your footing again with care that treats the whole picture. In-office neurofeedback has reasonable research support for calming a stress-revved nervous system, and we pair it with practical, honest support so you're not just pushing through one more week.

Learn more about neurofeedback → or explore the broader option of psychiatric care.

Veterans & first responders

Service leaves a mark. For veterans, active-duty service members, and first responders, that can mean hypervigilance that won't switch off, sleep that won't come, anger or numbness, or the quiet weight of things you've seen and done. Reaching out can feel like the hardest part.

As the spouse of an Army veteran and a member of The Headstrong Project, Courtney understands military life and offers discreet, trauma-informed care for service members, veterans, first responders, and their families across Hampton Roads. Please note Anchor Neuro is not in-network with TRICARE. Care can include Brainspotting, in-office neurofeedback, and psychiatric support built around your goals.

Life transitions

Big changes can shake you even when they're good ones: a new baby, a move or another PCS, a deployment or a homecoming, a divorce, a career shift, or stepping into a new season of life. It's normal to feel unsteady, anxious, or low while the ground moves under you.

We offer a steady place to think it through and a plan that meets you where you are. That can include supportive psychiatric care, and, when it fits, neurofeedback or Brainspotting to help your nervous system find its footing through the change.

Learn more about our psychiatric care →

Medication management

Whether you're starting a medication, rethinking one that isn't working, or just want a thoughtful set of eyes on your regimen, you deserve more than a refill and a wave goodbye. Medication can be genuinely helpful for the right person at the right time, and it isn't the answer for everyone.

We treat it as one tool, not a default. When medication makes sense, we explain why, what to expect, and the trade-offs, and we adjust based on how you respond rather than guessing. We prescribe only when it's clinically appropriate and after a full evaluation, we don't guarantee any specific medication, and you always stay part of the decision.
