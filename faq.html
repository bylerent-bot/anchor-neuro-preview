Questions, answered

Frequently asked questions

Straightforward answers about how we work, what care costs, and what to expect, so you can decide with confidence.

What happens in the free consult?

It is a short, no-pressure conversation to see whether we are the right fit for each other. It is not a clinical appointment, diagnosis, or medication visit. You complete a short, secure request form first, and we reach out to schedule.

How does medication management work?

It starts with a thorough psychiatric evaluation. If medication fits, we prescribe thoughtfully, start low and go slow, and follow up to see how you are responding, adjusting as we go. You stay part of every decision. We do not guarantee any specific medication or outcome, and we treat medication as one option within a plan rather than a one-size-fits-all answer.

Is medication required?

No. Medication is one option, not a requirement. Some people come for medication management, some for trauma care and therapy, and some for a combination. We build the plan around your goals, and you always have a say in whether medication is part of it.

Do you offer therapy?

Yes. Trauma care here is delivered through trauma-focused therapy, including Brainspotting, alongside medication support when it fits. Medication often works best paired with therapy. Depending on your needs, Courtney may provide therapy, coordinate it, or refer you to the right person.

Do you prescribe controlled substances or stimulants?

Prescribing decisions, including for controlled medications, are made individually after a clinical evaluation and follow Virginia and federal rules; some may require an in-person visit or added monitoring. We do not advertise or promise stimulants, benzodiazepines, or any specific controlled medication. If you are looking only for ongoing controlled-substance prescriptions, we may not be the right fit, and we will help point you toward appropriate care.

Do you accept insurance?

We accept insurance and self-pay. We are in-network with Aetna, Anthem / HealthKeepers, BlueCross BlueShield, Carelon, Cigna / Evernorth, and Sentara. We confirm your coverage or self-pay rate before you start, never as a surprise afterward.

Do you work with veterans and active-duty service members?

Yes. Anchor Neuro provides discreet, trauma-informed care for service members, veterans, and their families, and partners with The Headstrong Project. We understand military life and the discretion that trust requires.

Is my care confidential?

Your care is private and protected by law, and discretion matters deeply to us, especially for those who serve. There are narrow legal and safety exceptions that apply to any provider, and we are glad to walk you through exactly what is and is not shared before you begin.

Is telehealth available across Virginia?

Yes. We offer secure telehealth to anyone located in Virginia, along with in-person care in Virginia Beach. Medication management and therapy can be done by telehealth; neurofeedback is provided in the office.

What is neurofeedback, and is it safe?

Neurofeedback is a complementary, medication-free option some people choose to add alongside medication and therapy. It is non-invasive: sensors only read your brain's electrical activity; nothing is sent into you. It gives your brain gentle, real-time feedback so it can settle into calmer, more focused patterns over time.

How do I get started?

Complete a short, secure request form. We review every request personally and reach out to schedule your free consult, or help point you elsewhere if we are not the right fit.
