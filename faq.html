Questions, answered

Frequently asked questions

Straightforward answers about how we work, what care costs, and what to expect, so you can decide with confidence.

What happens in the free consult?

It is a short, no-pressure conversation to see whether we are the right fit for each other. It is not a clinical appointment, diagnosis, or medication visit. You complete a short, secure request form first, and we reach out to schedule.

Is neurofeedback safe?

Yes. Neurofeedback is non-invasive and medication-free. Sensors only read your brain's electrical activity; nothing is sent into you. It gives your brain gentle, real-time feedback so it can settle into calmer, more focused patterns over time.

Do you prescribe medication?

Yes, when it is clinically appropriate and after a full evaluation. Medication is one option alongside neurofeedback and integrative care, never a one-size-fits-all default. We do not guarantee any specific medication or provide early refills.

Do you accept insurance, and is neurofeedback covered?

We accept insurance and self-pay. We are in-network with Aetna, Anthem / HealthKeepers, BlueCross BlueShield, Carelon, Cigna / Evernorth, and Sentara. Coverage for neurofeedback varies; we confirm your coverage or self-pay rate before you start, never as a surprise afterward.

Do you work with veterans and active-duty service members?

Yes. Anchor Neuro provides discreet, trauma-informed care for service members, veterans, and their families, and partners with The Headstrong Project. We understand military life and the discretion that trust requires.

Is my care confidential?

Your care is private and protected by law, and discretion matters deeply to us, especially for those who serve. There are narrow legal and safety exceptions that apply to any provider, and we are glad to walk you through exactly what is and is not shared before you begin.

To confirm before launch (legal review): exact confidentiality wording, especially any active-duty / command-notification nuances.

Is telehealth available across Virginia?

Yes. We offer secure telehealth to anyone located in Virginia, along with in-person care in Virginia Beach. Neurofeedback is provided in the office; medication management and therapy can be done by telehealth.

How do I get started?

Complete a short, secure request form. We review every request personally and reach out to schedule your free consult, or help point you elsewhere if we are not the right fit.

Anchor Neuro is not a crisis or emergency service. If you are in crisis or thinking about harming yourself, call or text 988 (Suicide and Crisis Lifeline; veterans press 1) or go to your nearest emergency room.
