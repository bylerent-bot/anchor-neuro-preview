A different kind of evaluation

Care that treats the whole person, not a symptom list

A lot of psychiatric care moves fast: a few questions, a diagnosis, a prescription, and a follow-up months later. That is not how we work. At Anchor Neuro, your first visit starts with listening. We take time to understand your history, your stress, your sleep, your relationships, and what you actually want to feel different, then we build a plan around that.

You might be carrying anxiety you cannot put down, a low mood that has lingered too long, a mind that will not slow down, or the weight of something that happened years ago. Whatever brought you here, you deserve a plan that fits your life and your goals, not a one-size-fits-all path. That is the heart of integrative psychiatric care: looking at the whole person, and using every honest tool we have.

Medication, done thoughtfully

When medication helps, and when it doesn't

Medication can be life-changing for the right person at the right time, and it is not the answer for everyone. We treat it as one tool, not a default. When medication makes sense, we explain why, what to expect, and the trade-offs, and we adjust based on how you respond rather than guessing. When other approaches fit better, like neurofeedback, Brainspotting, or lifestyle support, we say so honestly. You always stay part of the decision.

We do prescribe medications, including controlled substances, but only when they are clinically appropriate and after a full evaluation. We do not guarantee any specific medication, do not provide early refills, and may request prior records as part of safe, responsible care. You can read the full medication and controlled-substance policy on our How it works page.

A quiet difference

We measure whether care is actually working

It is easy to lose track of progress when life is hard. So, when it is helpful, we use brief, validated check-ins like the PHQ-9 for depression or the GAD-7 for anxiety to see whether things are genuinely moving in the right direction over time. These take a minute or two, they are optional, and they are never a test you can pass or fail. They simply give us a clearer, honest picture so we can adjust your plan with real information instead of guesswork.

Psychiatric care FAQ

Do I have to take medication if I come to Anchor Neuro?

No. Medication is one tool, not the whole plan, and it is never a requirement. Some people benefit from it, some do well with neurofeedback, Brainspotting, or therapy-style support, and many do best with a combination. We talk through the options together and you stay in the driver's seat. If medication does make sense, we explain why, what to expect, and how we will track whether it is helping.

Do you offer psychiatric care by telehealth, or only in person?

Both. You can see us in person at our Virginia Beach office on Laskin Road, or by secure telehealth anywhere in Virginia, which is convenient for busy schedules and for military families managing deployments and moves. Neurofeedback is the one service that is in-office only, since it uses equipment on site. Medication management and evaluations work well over telehealth.

Will you prescribe controlled substances like stimulants for ADHD?

We prescribe medications, including controlled substances, only when they are clinically appropriate and after a full evaluation. We do not guarantee any specific medication and may request prior records as part of safe, responsible care. You can read the full medication and controlled-substance policy on our How it works page.

What does the first psychiatric visit actually look like?

It starts with listening. We take time to understand your history, your symptoms, your goals, and what has and has not worked before, rather than rushing to a label. From there we build a plan together, which may include medication, neurofeedback, Brainspotting, lifestyle support, or a combination. Your free consult comes first, so we can make sure we are the right fit before you ever book a clinical visit.
